MIDWEST REGION

The Midwest region of the National Parks System may not be as well-known as some of the other regions, but it offers visitors a chance to explore the unique landscapes and rich history of America's heartland. This region includes parks such as Isle Royale, Theodore Roosevelt, and Indiana Dunes, each with its own distinct character and natural beauty. Isle Royale, located in the northern reaches of Lake Superior, is a remote wilderness area accessible only by boat or seaplane, while Theodore Roosevelt offers visitors the opportunity to step back in time and explore the rugged badlands that inspired the 26th President. The Indiana Dunes, located on the southern shore of Lake Michigan, feature towering sand dunes, lush forests, and pristine beaches. Together, these parks showcase the diversity and resilience of the Midwest's natural and cultural heritage and offer visitors a chance to experience the region's unique character firsthand.
