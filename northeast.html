NORTHEAST REGION

The Northeast region of the National Parks System is a treasure trove of natural beauty, rich history, and cultural heritage. This region includes parks such as Acadia, the Cape Cod National Seashore, and the Statue of Liberty National Monument, each with its own unique character and charm. Acadia, located on the coast of Maine, features stunning vistas of the Atlantic Ocean, rugged coastlines, and beautiful forests, while the Cape Cod National Seashore offers visitors miles of pristine beaches, dunes, and marshes. The Statue of Liberty National Monument, located in New York Harbor, is one of the most recognizable symbols of America and a powerful reminder of the country's commitment to freedom and democracy. Other parks in the region, such as the Minute Man National Historical Park in Massachusetts and the Gettysburg National Military Park in Pennsylvania, offer visitors a chance to explore the region's rich history and cultural heritage, from the Revolutionary War to the Civil War and beyond. Together, these parks showcase the natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical significance of the Northeast and offer visitors a chance to connect with this unique region.
